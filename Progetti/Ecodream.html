Overview

"Ecodream has a lot of potential and a powerful actual concept, but it needs to be unlocked with a better user experience and a more powerful design!"

Ecodream is a sustainable brand that aims to produce eco-friendly bags and accessories while promoting environmental responsibility. Its strenghts lie in the message they want to communicate, in their local production and the uniqueness and quality of their products. However, the current e-commerce website has some usability and accessibility issues that make it difficult to navigate and use and the design needs to be improved to better reflect the brand's values. The goal of this project is to redesign the whole e-commerce experience without losing the essence of the brand.

Research

What I did

-- Heuristic and Accessibility Analysis

  • I conducted a heuristic evaluation based on Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics to identify usability issues affecting navigation, product discovery and overall user experience, across both desktop and mobile devices.
  • I evaluated the existing site across five usability dimensions: Learnability, Efficiency, Memorability, Errors and Satisfaction. Each dimension was rated based on observed behaviour and friction points found during the audit.

What I discovered

  1. 01

    Users can't orient themselves. No breadcrumbs, poor navigation, no search bar. They don't know where they are or how to find what they need.

  2. 02

    Navigation creates constant friction. Product listing with no filters and key pages hard to reach. Too many confusing steps.

  3. 03

    Calls to action are invisible or ambiguous. Clickable images with no indication, unclear CTAs. Users don't know what to do next.

  4. 04

    Information is scattered and hard to scan. Excessive unformatted text, important info buried in wrong pages, same content repeated across pages.

  5. 05

    The brand story doesn't build trust. Sustainability values are highly present but poorly communicated: not specific, not visible enough, not well organized.

-- Competitor analysis

I analysed direct and indirect competitors to understand how sustainable fashion brands organise product information, communicate their values and guide users through the shopping experience.

"I wanted to understand how competing brands address similar user needs and identify best practices that could be adapted to improve the EcoDream experience."

-- User survey

Before designing for users, I needed to understand them.
I ran a survey covering demographics, sustainability awareness, budget range and shopping preferences. I also asked specific questions about the existing Ecodream shop: filter availability, product information clarity and navigation and explored what actually stops users from completing a purchase.

Question: Do you ever abandon your cart before completing a purchase? Why?

-- User personas + journey

Based on the data collected, I defined 3 user personas to identify a clear target audience. For each persona, I mapped a user journey to simulate realistic purchase and navigation experiences on the existing site, highlighting the key friction points along the way.

02

Strategy

Ecodream doesn't sell essential goods. As the survey revealed, most users abandon their cart simply because the purchase feels unnecessary — not because of the site itself. Navigation issues and lack of product information come second. But that's exactly the point: we can't control personal decisions or budgets, but we can make sure that a confusing navigation or poor communication are never the reason someone walks away. Why would anyone spend time on a poorly organised site when the competition is just one click away? The goal of this strategy was to remove every obstacle that had nothing to do with the product itself.

Main friction points

  1. 01

    Users struggled to find key product information quickly.

  2. 02

    The website structure made navigation and product discovery less intuitive.

  3. 03

    The brand values were not communicated clearly enough during the shopping experience.

Strategic decisions

  1. 01

    Improve the information architecture to make content easier to find.

  2. 02

    Clarify product information, sustainability values and purchase-related details.

  3. 03

    Use a stronger visual hierarchy to guide users through the experience.

-- Reorganising information architecture

I reorganised the website structure to make navigation clearer, reduce friction and help users find the most relevant information faster.

-- Reorganising texts and general structure

I worked on simplifying the content structure, making key information more visible and improving the hierarchy across the main pages.

  • Clearer product information hierarchy
  • More visible sustainability messages
  • Better separation between product, brand and support content
  • More consistent page sections and content blocks

-- How might we...

I reframed the main problems into design opportunities to guide the next phase of the project.
