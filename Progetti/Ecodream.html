Overview

"Ecodream has a lot of potential and a powerful actual concept, but it needs to be unlocked with a better user experience and a more powerful design!"

Ecodream is a sustainable brand that aims to produce eco-friendly bags and accessories while promoting environmental responsibility. Its strenghts lie in the message they want to communicate, in their local production and the uniqueness and quality of their products. However, the current e-commerce website has some usability and accessibility issues that make it difficult to navigate and use and the design needs to be improved to better reflect the brand's values. The goal of this project is to redesign the whole e-commerce experience without losing the essence of the brand.

I explored the brand to understand its direction, values and user needs. I analysed competitors to identify patterns in the industry, and audited the existing site for usability and accessibility issues, mapping where and why users were struggling.

What I did

-- Euristhic and Accessibility Analysis

  • I conducted a heuristic evaluation based on Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics to identify usability issues affecting navigation, product discovery and overall user experience, across both desktop and mobile devices.

  • I evaluated the existing site across five usability dimensions: Learnability, Efficiency, Memorability, Errors and Satisfaction. Each dimension was rated based on observed behaviour and friction points found during the audit.

What I discovered

  1. 01

    Users need clear product information before feeling confident enough to buy.

  2. 02

    Sustainability messages must be transparent, specific and easy to understand.

  3. 03

    The navigation should guide users quickly toward products, categories and key information.

  4. 04

    Trust elements are essential when the brand communicates ethical values.

  5. 05

    The purchase flow should reduce friction and avoid unnecessary decision fatigue.

  6. 06

    Accessibility improvements can make the experience more inclusive and easier to use.
