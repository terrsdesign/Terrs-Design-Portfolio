Overview

"Ecodream has a lot of potential and a powerful actual concept, but it needs to be unlocked with a better user experience and a more powerful design!"

Ecodream is a sustainable brand that aims to produce eco-friendly bags and accessories while promoting environmental responsibility. Its strenghts lies in the message they want to communicate, in their local production and the uniqueness and quality of their products. However, the current e-commerce website has some usability and accessibility issues that make it difficult to navigate and use and the design needs to be improved to better reflect the brand's values. The goal of this project is to redesign the whole e-commerce experience without losing the essence of the brand.

The process for Ecodream

01
⌕

Research

I explored the brand to understand its direction, values and user needs. I analysed competitors to identify patterns in the industry, and audited the existing site for usability and accessibility issues, mapping where and why users were struggling.

02
✦

Strategy

After the research phase, I mapped out the steps needed to improve Ecodream's experience, prioritising the most critical issues first. From there, I worked on rebuilding the site's structure and visual direction, keeping the brand's original identity intact.

↓ Jump to
03
◧

Design

I started with wireframes to immediately visualise the new site structure. Once the layout felt right, I built a complete design system covering palette, icons, typography and interactive components. From there, I moved into high-fidelity screens.

↓ Jump to
04
▶

Prototype

Once all the screens were ready, I connected them into an interactive prototype and tested it with real users. I ran an unmoderated remote usability test using Maze, involving 7 participants.

↓ Jump to
05
✓

Conclusion

The testing phase validated some decisions and challenged others, revealing new improvements to implement. Some changes were made immediately, others noted for the next iteration.
